THINGS TO KNOW

How to get there

Camarines Norte can be easily reached within 7 to 8 hours from Manila by bus. You can book a ticket in either Cubao or Pasay bus terminals. Bus campanies that can take you to Daet are: Philtranco, Superlines, DLTB and Amihan.

By plane you can book a 45 minutes flight to Naga, Camarines Sur via Pili Airport through Cebu Pacific or Philippine Airlines. From the airport you can ride a UV Express Van for 2 hours drive to Daet

How to get there

When you are in Daet, you can ride a commuter van, jeepney, or bus in getting around the beautiful spots in other municipalities of Camarines Norte. Tricyc are also abundant and can easily take you to nearby locations around Daet. Taxi is available in Daet. You can text or call OLA TAXI 0918-624-2924 / 0995-552-0633 for pick-up and reservations.

Where to stay

Hotels and lodging houses are abundant and conveniently located in Daet, and through the province. These are budget friendly and reasonably priced. For those who adventure seekers, you can always bring a tent and camp at nearby beach, falls or rivers, Private homes in various spots offer Homestay, for the most authentic experience of local culture and lifestyle.

Guided Tours

You may request for tour packages from local travel agencies. Accredited local tour guides are available from Tourism Officers of different municipalities. They are friendly, reliable, and knows the locality like the palm of their hand. You may also drop by at the Camarines Norte Provincial Tourism Office for more information.

Internet

Most hotels and restaurants have Wi-Fi connections. For hotels just ask the receptionist for the password after you check-in. Most of them offer it only in their lobbies so you need your own connections if you want access in your room. For restaurants, you can ask the password at the counter or the waiter after you order. All local mobile services cover most of the province with 3G and LTE data connectivity.

Language

No language barrier. Tagalog or Filipino language is widely spoken by the people of Cam. Norte. In fact, even in Bicol speaking town like Daet, Tagalog is the lingua Franca in radio. TV, local newspaper and even in church service. Most CamNorteños can speak Filipino without a heavy accent so they can easily be understood. Like most Flipinos they can also easily converse in English.
