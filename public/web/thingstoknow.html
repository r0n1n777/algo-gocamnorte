THINGS TO KNOW

Camarines Norte

At the crossroads of Southern Tagalog and Bicol Region lies Camarines Norte or fondly called CamNorte. It is located just 300 kilometers south of Metro Manila. Rich bodies of water such as the Pacific Ocean, San Miguel Bay as well as the fertile soil of Quezon and Camarines Sur provinces surrounds this Tourism Gem.

The Spanish conquistador Juan de Salcedo named it "Camarines which means "storehouse" owing to its numerous barns in its farms and riverbanks during that time. The name perfectly described its rich heritage and natural resources: It is indeed a storehouse of treasures. Now is the time to discover this tourism’s best kept secret. It is abundant in mostly untouched beautiful spots. You will find out that it is a unique blend of Tagalog and Bicol Cultures. This makes CamNorte "Uniquely Tagalog and Uniquely Bicol"

It is composed of 12 municipalities grouped into two congressional districts-the Tagalog Speaking composed of Labo, Capalonga, Sta. Elena, Paracale, and Jose Panganiban; and the Bicol Speaking composed of Daet, Talisay, Vinzons, Mercedes, Basud, San Vicente and San Lorenzo Ruiz.

For a long time, the province kept its beautiful spots because it is always devastated by strong typhoons. In the past year’s typhoons change its usual course and mostly spared the province. Agriculture and its natural resources now regenerated and blossomed. CamNorte is ready for your visit anytime of the year.

Come and visit the new pride of CamNorte, the Calaguas Island in Vinzons. Isla Lipana & Co, named it as the “Top Philippine Tourism Gem” Based on an online poll.

Come to CamNorte, come with a purpose: conquer your fear, wash away your blues, embrace mother earth, discover past, celebrate life, uplift your spirit, indulged in gastronomic delight, nourish your body and connect with amazing people. These are the things that CamNorte can offer. “Uniquely Bicol, Uniquely Beautiful”.

How to get there

Camarines Norte can be easily reached within 7 to 8 hours from Manila by bus. You can book a ticket in either Cubao or Pasay bus terminals. Bus campanies that can take you to Daet are: Philtranco, Superlines, DLTB and Amihan. By plane you can book a 45 minutes flight to Naga, Camarines Sur via Pili Airport through Cebu Pacific or Philippine Airlines. From the airport you can ride a UV Express Van for 2 hours drive to Daet.

How to get there

When you are in Daet, you can ride a commuter van, jeepney, or bus in getting around the beautiful spots in other municipalities of Camarines Norte. Tricyc are also abundant and can easily take you to nearby locations around Daet. Taxi is available in Daet. You can text or call OLA TAXI 0918-624-2924 / 0995-552-0633 for pick-up and reservations.

Where to stay

Hotels and lodging houses are abundant and conveniently located in Daet, and through the province. These are budget friendly and reasonably priced. For those who adventure seekers, you can always bring a tent and camp at nearby beach, falls or rivers, Private homes in various spots offer Homestay, for the most authentic experience of local culture and lifestyle.

Guided Tours

You may request for tour packages from local travel agencies. Accredited local tour guides are available from Tourism Officers of different municipalities. They are friendly, reliable, and knows the locality like the palm of their hand. You may also drop by at the Camarines Norte Provincial Tourism Office for more information.

Internet

Most hotels and restaurants have Wi-Fi connections. For hotels just ask the receptionist for the password after you check-in. Most of them offer it only in their lobbies so you need your own connections if you want access in your room. For restaurants, you can ask the password at the counter or the waiter after you order. All local mobile services cover most of the province with 3G and LTE data connectivity.

Language

No language barrier. Tagalog or Filipino language is widely spoken by the people of Cam. Norte. In fact, even in Bicol speaking town like Daet, Tagalog is the lingua Franca in radio. TV, local newspaper and even in church service. Most CamNorteños can speak Filipino without a heavy accent so they can easily be understood. Like most Flipinos they can also easily converse in English.

Wenceslao Quinito Vinzons

Wenceslao "Bintao" Quinito Vinzons was a Filipino patriot and leader of the Philippine armed resistance against the Japanese invasion in World War II. He was the youngest delegate to the 1935 Philippine Constitutional Convention.

Born: September 28, 1910 in Vinzons, Camarines Norte

Nationality: Philippine

Assisanated: Daet, July 15, 1942

Education: University of The Philippine System

Jose Maria Panganiban

Jose Maria Panganiban y Enverga was a Filipino propagandist, linguist, and essayist. He is one of the main writers and contributors for La Solidaridad, writing under the pen names "Jomapa" and "J.M.P."

Born: February 1, 1863, Jose Panganiban, Camarines Norte

Nationality: Philippine

Died: August 19, 1890, Barcelona, Spain

Monuments: Jose Panganiban, Daet, and Naga, Camarines Sur

Organization: La Solidaridad, Asociacion Hispano-Filipina, Free Masonry

Fernando Amorsolo y Cueto

Fernando Amorsolo y Cueto, was the first National Artist of the Philippine, traces his roots to the town of Daet, San Vicemte, and Vinzons. It was believed that his precious artworks, which depict rural farming scenes, were inspired by the vast agricultural lands of Camarines Norte.

Born: May 30, 1892, Paco, Manila

Died: April 24, 1972, Quezon City

Nationality: Philippine

Award: National Artist of the Philippines for Visual ArtsDaet, July 15, 1942
